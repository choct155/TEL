Exploring Data with Simulation¶

Among other things, simulation has demonstrated the capacity to provide insight into small sample sizes. To this end, this will work through some examples to provide basic concepts.

The initial problem is determining the fairness of a flipped coin after only 8 flips (6H:2T). To evaluate this, we are going to simulate a sequence of tosses at multiple levels of p (where p=.5 is a balanced coin).

import random
import numpy as np
import pandas as pd
from pandas import Series,DataFrame

plt.rcParams['figure.figsize']=10,10

#Define trial structure
repeats,tosses=60,8

#Define function representing a toss of heads
def heads(tosses,p):
    h=0
    #For each toss, heads occurs if the random number is below p
    for x in range(0,tosses):
        if random.random() < p: 
            h+=1
    return h

#Create containers to capture results for plotting
prob=[]
h_t=[]

#Initialize probability value
p=0

#Roll through each probability value at .05 intervals and grab number of heads
while p<1.01:
    for t in range(0,repeats):
        #print p,'\t',heads(tosses,p)
        prob.append(p)
        h_t.append(heads(tosses,p))
    p+=.05

#Set jitter parameters to deal with overplotting
jitter=.01
jitter2=.2
prob_jitter=[p+random.uniform(-jitter,jitter) for p in prob]
ht_jitter=[ht+random.uniform(-jitter2,jitter2) for ht in h_t]

#Plot results
plt.scatter(prob_jitter,ht_jitter,alpha=.2,facecolors='None')
plt.xlim([0-jitter,1+jitter])
plt.ylim([0-jitter2,8+jitter2])

What about this bootstrapping business? One of the issues we have in the District (like most places) is the sparse nature of PUMS data. Perhaps we could resample to get more out of this data? Let's take a look at an example. We are going to test resampling against a known distribution. The data (n=25) are taken from a standard Gaussian distribution with mean zero and standard deviation of one. We know at the outset that the standard error (standard deviation divided by the square of the number of observations) is 1/5.
